Healthcare API Reliability & Monitoring

Built monitoring and observability systems for production-grade backend services handling millions of healthcare requests.

The Business Context

Healthcare workflows depend on production-grade backend services that need to be available and responsive. When APIs are slow or unavailable, it affects patient care, provider operations, and business processes. Teams needed visibility into system health and the ability to identify issues before they impacted users.

The Constraints

Scalable systems handled millions of requests per month across distributed infrastructure. Issues could originate from multiple sources like application code, infrastructure, dependencies, or data problems. Teams needed to detect problems quickly but also needed context to understand root causes. Alert fatigue was a real concern because too many alerts and teams would ignore them.

The Approach

Built monitoring dashboards that tracked key metrics including request volume, latency, error rates, and dependency health. Implemented alerting that focused on business impact rather than raw metrics, reducing noise while improving detection speed. Created runbooks that documented common issues and resolution steps, helping teams respond faster to incidents. Conducted root-cause analysis on production issues to identify patterns and prevent recurrence.

The Outcome

Reduced mean time to detection by providing clear visibility into system health. Helped teams resolve incidents faster through better context and documentation. Improved system reliability by identifying and addressing recurring issues.

Event-Driven Data Pipeline for Healthcare Operations

Built real-time data pipelines to process continuous streams of healthcare events across distributed systems.

The Business Context

Healthcare operations generate continuous streams of events like claims submissions, eligibility checks, and provider updates. Teams needed to process these events in real-time through production-critical applications to support operational workflows and maintain data consistency across systems.

The Constraints

Event volumes were high and variable. Processing needed to be reliable because lost or duplicated events could cause data inconsistencies. Different downstream systems had different latency requirements. The pipeline needed to handle schema changes without breaking existing consumers.

The Approach

Implemented event streaming using Kafka to handle high-throughput, real-time data flows. Built processing logic that validated events, enriched data, and routed to appropriate downstream systems. Designed for fault tolerance with proper error handling and retry mechanisms. Created monitoring to track processing lag and identify bottlenecks.

The Outcome

Enabled real-time data processing that supported operational workflows. Improved data consistency across systems through reliable event handling. Provided visibility into data flows that helped teams troubleshoot issues faster.

CI/CD Pipeline Automation for Regulated Environment

Automated deployment processes in a healthcare environment where changes needed to be both fast and compliant.

The Business Context

In healthcare, deployments need to be reliable, traceable, and compliant with regulatory requirements. Manual deployment processes were time-consuming and introduced risk through configuration errors, missed steps, and lack of audit trails.

The Constraints

Changes needed to be tested thoroughly before reaching production. Deployments needed to be repeatable and auditable. Rollback procedures needed to be fast and reliable. The process needed to balance speed with safety because teams wanted to deploy frequently but couldn't compromise on quality or compliance.

The Approach

Automated build, test, and deployment pipelines using Jenkins and containerization with Docker and Kubernetes. Integrated unit and integration testing into CI workflows to catch issues early. Implemented automated validation checks for configuration and compliance requirements. Created clear deployment documentation and runbooks for troubleshooting.

The Outcome

Reduced manual deployment effort and accelerated release cycles. Decreased post-release defects through automated testing. Improved audit compliance through automated tracking of changes and deployments. Enabled teams to deploy more frequently with greater confidence.

Promotional Campaign Effectiveness Analysis

Analyzed promotional campaigns to separate genuine impact from noise like seasonality and external events.

The Business Context

The lottery ran multiple promotional campaigns throughout the year, but it wasn't always clear which promotions were driving incremental sales versus simply shifting timing of purchases. Leadership needed to understand ROI to make better decisions about promotional spend.

The Constraints

Sales were influenced by many factors like seasonality, jackpot sizes, external events, and promotions. Customer behavior varied significantly across segments. Data came from multiple systems with different levels of granularity. Stakeholders needed answers quickly to inform upcoming campaign decisions.

The Approach

Designed experiment-style analyses to isolate promotional impact from other factors. Built models to estimate redemption likelihood and expected sales uplift. Segmented customers to identify which groups responded to different promotion types. Created dashboards that showed campaign performance metrics like conversion rates, incremental sales, and ROI in a way that supported decision-making.

The Outcome

Helped leadership identify underperforming promotions and reallocate budget to more effective campaigns. Provided insights into which customer segments were most responsive to different promotion types. Enabled faster, more informed decisions about promotional strategy.

Credit Risk Assessment and Portfolio Monitoring

Built models to assess borrower risk and support portfolio management decisions across different regions and customer segments.

The Business Context

Ford needed to understand credit risk across its auto loan portfolio to make informed decisions about lending criteria, collections strategies, and capital allocation. Risk patterns varied by region, customer segment, and loan characteristics.

The Constraints

Credit data came from multiple sources with varying quality and timeliness. Models needed to perform consistently across different geographies and customer segments. Risk assessments needed to be explainable to support business decisions. Stakeholders needed timely visibility into portfolio health without manual data pulls.

The Approach

Built analytical models to estimate delinquency risk and probability of default using credit scores, repayment history, and borrower characteristics. Validated model performance across different segments using precision, recall, and ROC-AUC metrics. Segmented borrowers into risk tiers to support targeted monitoring and collections strategies. Created dashboards that communicated risk trends to finance and operations teams.

The Outcome

Enabled more informed decisions about lending criteria and collections resource allocation. Reduced time spent on manual reporting, allowing teams to focus on analysis and action. Improved visibility into portfolio risk trends across regions and segments.

Enterprise Data Model for Cross-Functional Analytics

Designed a consolidated data model that integrated information from dozens of source systems to support consistent reporting and analysis.

The Business Context

Teams across the organization needed consistent access to data for reporting and analysis, but information was scattered across multiple systems with different structures and definitions. This led to inconsistent metrics, duplicated effort, and slow decision-making.

The Constraints

Data came from 40+ source systems with different schemas and update frequencies. Different teams had different definitions for similar metrics. The data model needed to support both operational reporting and analytical use cases. Changes to source systems couldn't break existing reports and dashboards.

Worked with stakeholders to establish consistent metric definitions and business rules. Built validation checks to ensure data quality and flag inconsistencies. Used LLM-based agents to validate and standardize address data from inconsistent sources, improving data quality while reducing manual review effort. Created documentation that helped teams understand what data was available and how to use it correctly.

The Outcome

Enabled consistent reporting and analysis across teams. Reduced time spent reconciling conflicting numbers. Improved decision-making speed by providing reliable, accessible data. Freed up analytical capacity by reducing duplicated data preparation work.

Workflow Automation for Enterprise Operations

Identified and automated repetitive manual tasks across enterprise workflows to free up team capacity for work requiring judgment.

The Business Context

Teams were spending significant time on manual, repetitive tasks like data entry, report generation, status updates, and approvals. This created bottlenecks, introduced errors, and prevented teams from focusing on work that required judgment and expertise.

The Constraints

Workflows spanned multiple systems that didn't integrate well. Different teams had different processes for similar tasks. Some steps required human judgment and couldn't be fully automated. Changes to workflows needed to be documented and approved. Teams were busy and had limited time to adopt new tools.

The Approach

Built automation that handled routine tasks while preserving human oversight for complex cases. Used LLM APIs to validate and standardize address data from inconsistent formats, reducing manual review time while maintaining accuracy through validation rules. Designed solutions that integrated with existing systems rather than requiring wholesale replacement. Created clear documentation and provided training to help teams adopt new processes.

The Outcome

Reduced turnaround time for routine processes by eliminating manual steps. Freed up team capacity to focus on work requiring judgment and expertise. Improved consistency and reduced errors through automation. Quantified impact by measuring reductions in processing time and manual effort.

The Business Context

Organized documentation to support efficient audit responses.

How I Use AI

Using AI deliberately to improve efficiency while maintaining judgment and transparency.

I use AI when it meaningfully improves how I work, particularly in making sense of ambiguity, accelerating early analysis, or surfacing patterns in data that would otherwise take longer to uncover. As AI capabilities continue to evolve, I see it as a powerful way to improve efficiency and broaden perspective in the early stages of problem-solving.

I'm deliberate about where I apply it. When simpler analytical approaches are more reliable, or when explainability and transparency are critical, I rely on traditional methods and human judgment. In high-stakes or regulated contexts, AI supports analysis rather than driving conclusions, and outputs are always validated and documented.

AI is a tool, not a substitute for thinking. Its value lies in helping me move faster, ask better questions, and identify angles I might have missed, while the core work remains the same: understanding the problem, questioning the data, and translating insights into decisions that people can trust and act on.
